Past:

ACER
(Automated Card-Playing Entertainment Robot)

ACER was my senior design project for my BS of Computer Science at KU.
ACER is a software for Aldebaran's NAO robots that allows it to play simple card games with multiple human opponents.
Mainly created with python code, me and my team worked for a total of 10 months on this project for our Senior Design class and the University of Kansas. We were able to use Aldebaran's NAO robots and the associated interface, Choregraphe, in our project. The NAO robot has computer vision modules built in, as well as prehensile fingers and the ability to speak and interpret language. We were able to use and manipulate all of these features in tandem, along with python programs, to bring our project to life.

The end result was a success, and we created our robot, ACER, that was successful in playing card games with human opponents. The two games we were able to implement were Crazy-8s and Black Jack, although with the framework we put in place, it would be very simple to add more card games to ACER's arsenal.
